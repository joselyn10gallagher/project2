History of Special Effects

Before CGI, film makers had to be creative in order to create the world they wanted to have in their movies. As said before, the techniques film makers use/d are props, stop motion, paintings, puppets, and make-up.

  1. Drawing Animation

    Using the single-frame method (single images projected at a high volume of frames per second), comic illustrations overlap each other to create moving images.

  2. Miniatures

    Detailed miniature models of full-scale cityscapes were used alongside perspective techniques to create otherwise nonexistent environments.

  1. Paintings

    Matte paintings were actual projections or paintings placed behind foreground objects to trick audiences into believing the actors were in a different location.

  2. Stop Motion

    Realistic puppets or models that are manipulated and photographed one frame at a time.

  3. Doubling

    Using split-screen technology, the actor is in the same scene twice, were filmmakers locked the camera in position and shot the scene a second time, laying half of each shot onto a single negative to create a composite.

  4. Makeup

    Actors wear prosthetics that change their facial/body features to create another character.
